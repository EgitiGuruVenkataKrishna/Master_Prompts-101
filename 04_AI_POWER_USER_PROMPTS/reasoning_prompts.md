# Reasoning Prompts

Reasoning prompts encourage the model to **think through problems carefully**.

They improve results for:

• complex questions
• analytical tasks
• decision-making

---

# 1. Step-by-Step Reasoning

Act as a logical problem solver.

Problem:
[PROBLEM]

Instructions:

1. Understand the problem
2. Identify relevant information
3. Solve the problem step by step
4. Present the final result

Output:

Step-by-step reasoning followed by the answer.

---

# 2. Decision Analysis Prompt

Act as a strategic decision advisor.

Decision Problem:
[PROBLEM]

Tasks:

1. Identify possible options
2. Evaluate pros and cons
3. Consider risks
4. Recommend the best option

Output:

Options
Pros and Cons
Risk Analysis
Recommendation

---

# 3. Analytical Comparison Prompt

Act as an analyst.

Compare the following:

[ITEM A] vs [ITEM B]

Evaluation Criteria:

1. Strengths
2. Weaknesses
3. Use cases
4. Limitations

Output:
Structured comparison table.

---

# 4. Hypothesis Evaluation Prompt

Act as a critical thinker.

Hypothesis:
[HYPOTHESIS]

Tasks:

1. Examine the hypothesis
2. Identify assumptions
3. Evaluate supporting evidence
4. Identify possible weaknesses

Output:

Balanced evaluation of the hypothesis.
